GPA Calculation Rules

LEPL Ilia State University - GPA Calculation Rule

  1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) is the quotient of the sum of the products of the GP (Grade Point) earned in each course and the credits earned for that course, divided by the total number of credits of the courses passed by the student.
  2. GP (Grade Point) is the converted value of a course score on a 4-point scale.
  3. The score categories in the GP system are: F - 0-50 points (0 points, Fail), E - 51-60 points, D - 61-70 points, C - 71-80 points, B - 81-90 points, A - 91-95 points, and A+ - 96-100 points.
  4. In the GP system, an E category score (51-60) is fixed at 1.00 GP. Scores in the D, C, B, and A categories (61 through 95 inclusive) increase evenly by 1/12, approximately 0.08, for each point. An A+ category score (96-100) is fixed at 4.00 GP.
  5. GPA is issued for all academic programs at Ilia State University, except doctoral-level programs. GPA is also not issued for certificate programs.
  6. Only the final GPA is issued at Ilia State University and it is reflected in the diploma supplement. This rule does not apply to the one-cycle Medical Doctor program.
  7. When the final GPA is calculated, fractional values are rounded to one decimal place.
GPA = Σ(GP × Credit) / Σ(Credit)
